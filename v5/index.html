~ The Acts ~

Act I — The New York Treasury (Summer 1625—err, 2025)

[Scene: The New York City Department of Finance, Property Valuation & Tax Mapping]

As Data Science Graduate Intern, our hero did forge interactive Python Dash applications most wondrous, transforming administrative spatial data into instruments for the valuation of high-end properties. He conjured dashboards presenting city-wide and borough-level trends in property tax-to-income ratios for the eyes of senior management alone. Furthermore, he did establish automated CI/CD workflows in GitHub Actions, streamlining the periodic querying of geospatial data from the ESRI API—a feat of modern sorcery.

Act II — The World Bank of Verona (March 1623—August 1624, i.e. 2023–2024)

[Scene: Office of the Chief Economist, Latin America and the Caribbean]

Serving as Research Analyst, Pablo did develop an R Shiny application of great cunning, computing non-salary labour costs across eleven countries in the realm of LAC. He conducted the querying, processing, harmonization, and visualization of eleven national household surveys—a Herculean labour of data. He did synthesize recent labour and tax regulations into itemized spreadsheets, and co-authored one of LCRCE’s most celebrated flagship reports on competition, trade liberalization, and antitrust enforcement. He furthermore compiled evidence from over one thousand research papers into systematic literature reviews—truly, a library worthy of Prospero himself.

Act III — The United Nations, First Campaign (April 1622–February 1623, i.e. 2022–2023)

[Scene: UNDP, SDG and Development Policy, New York]

As Economic Researcher, he did build a web-based R Shiny application to produce country reports through real-time querying of macroeconomic and human development data for all five-and-twenty nations in the portfolio. He led the development of automated tools to disseminate findings from two-and-twenty high-frequency phone surveys on the impact of COVID-19 upon household welfare. And lo, he did co-author the joint UNDP-IDB report “A Better World for the Migrant Population in Latin America and the Caribbean.”

Act IV — The United Nations, Early Days (November 1620–March 1622, i.e. 2020–2022)

[Scene: UNDP, SDG and Development Policy, performed remotely]

In his youth as Junior Economist, Pablo did process and analyze seventeen public opinion surveys to study perceptions of income inequality, fiscal fairness, and redistribution across the region. He co-authored Chapter the Fourth of the 2021 UNDP LAC Regional Human Development Report—“Trapped: High Inequality and Low Growth”—examining the dark bonds between violence and inequality. He performed comprehensive data harmonization in Stata, R, and VBA of four-and-twenty phone surveys, delivering timely insights to policymakers most grateful.
