一
FIRST STATION
THE TRAVELLER'S ORIGIN

The traveller Pablo Hernandez Pedraza first studied the way of numbers and the way of people at the Universidad de los Andes in Bogota, earning twin scrolls in Economics and Anthropology in the autumn of 2020.

He journeyed far north to the great academy of Chicago, the Harris School of Public Policy, where he now pursues the advanced arts of Computational Analysis & Public Policy. He studies Machine Learning, the language arts of NLP, and the construction of LLM Agents. He instructs younger travellers in the Python discipline and guides the Data Science Clinic.

The traveller commands both the Spanish and English tongues.

二
SECOND STATION
THE FOUR PROVINCES
四

The Treasury of New York

Data Science Graduate Intern — NYC Dept. of Finance — Summer 2025

In the great city, the traveller forged Python Dash instruments for the valuation of high properties from administrative spatial records. He rendered dashboards of tax-to-income ratios for the city elders and established automated CI/CD pathways through GitHub Actions, drawing geospatial knowledge from the ESRI oracle at appointed intervals.

三

The World Treasury of Nations

Research Analyst — World Bank, LAC Chief Economist — 2023–2024

Across eleven provinces of Latin America, the traveller crafted an R Shiny instrument computing non-salary labour costs for the universe of formal enterprise. He harmonized eleven household surveys—a great labour of unification. He compiled the tax and labour decrees of eleven lands into ordered scrolls, co-inscribed the LCRCE flagship manuscript on competition and trade, and gathered over one thousand scholarly papers into systematic catalogues.

二

The United Nations, Second Posting

Economic Researcher — UNDP, SDG & Development Policy — 2022–2023

The traveller built an R Shiny instrument that produced country scrolls for five-and-twenty nations through real-time consultation of macroeconomic and human development records. He led the dissemination of findings from two-and-twenty high-frequency surveys on the great plague's impact upon household welfare. He co-inscribed the UNDP-IDB manuscript on the migrant populations, informed by nine controlled trials.

一

The United Nations, First Posting

Junior Economist — UNDP, SDG & Development Policy — 2020–2022

In the earliest chapter, the traveller processed seventeen opinion surveys on inequality, fiscal fairness, and redistribution across the region. He co-inscribed Chapter the Fourth of the 2021 Regional Human Development Report—“Trapped”—on the entanglement of violence and inequality. He harmonized four-and-twenty phone surveys in the dialects of Stata, R, and VBA, delivering counsel to the policymakers of many lands.
